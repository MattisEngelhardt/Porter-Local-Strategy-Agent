# recruiting_screening_playbook.md — Screening CVs Against a Job Profile

> The methodology for Porter's **Analyst dimension (Recruiting)**: read candidate CVs *extremely
> carefully* and score them against a job profile so a recruiter gets a defensible, ranked
> shortlist in minutes. Local and DSGVO-safe — CVs never leave the machine. **The human decides;**
> Porter produces evidence and a ranking, never a hiring decision.

## The Cardinal Rule: Zero Hallucination
- Score **only** what the CV actually states. If a requirement is not in the CV, it is not met —
  full stop. Never assume a skill from a job title, a school, or an employer's reputation.
- Every **non-zero** score needs **evidence**: a short verbatim quote from the CV. No quote → the
  score is low and the evidence field is empty.
- Never invent or inflate experience, dates, or seniority. Quote facts as written.
- Anything the CV does not answer is an explicit **gap**, not a guess.

## Fairness & Bias Avoidance
- Judge **job-relevant** evidence only: skills, experience, measurable results, qualifications.
- Do **not** score on name, gender, age, nationality, photo, address, marital status, or any
  protected attribute. Ignore them entirely — they must not influence any score.
- Apply the same criteria identically to every candidate.

## How to Score
- Score each criterion **0-100**: 0 = no support in the CV, ~50 = partial/adjacent, ~80+ = strong
  direct evidence. Be consistent across candidates.
- The **weighted total** uses the criteria weights — the few criteria that matter most dominate.
- Give a one-sentence **rationale** per criterion and a 2-3 sentence **fit summary** per candidate.
- List concrete **strengths** and **gaps** (each gap = a requirement not evidenced in the CV).

## Read Like a Recruiter
- Identify the candidate's actual experience, skills, and measurable impact — separate real
  achievements from buzzwords.
- Note recency and depth (a skill used for 5 years ≠ a skill listed once).
- Flag ambiguities (unclear dates, gaps in employment) as gaps, not as assumptions.

## Output
- A ranked table: best-fit first, with the weighted score, per-criterion scores, and a short
  summary + gaps per candidate.
- The recruiter reviews evidence and makes the call. Porter's job is to make that review fast and
  grounded — every score traceable to the CV.

## Language
Match the language of the job profile / CVs (DE in → DE out, EN in → EN out). Keep established
role/skill terms as written.
